Frequently Asked Questions

How does the fare calculator know which zone a stop is in?

Each stop is stored in a database along with its assigned zone number. When you enter a stop, the calculator searches the database, retrieves its zone, and uses that information to calculate the correct fare.

How are the fares calculated?

The fare is determined based on the highest zone travelled. For example, travelling from Zone 1 to Zone 3 results in a Zone 3 fare. If both stops are in Zone 1, the Zone 1 fare applies.

Do I need to enter a destination?

No. If no destination is entered, the calculator assumes travel within the origin stop's zone and calculates the fare accordingly.

Do I need to use the full name for a railway station?

No. If you enter "Heuston" instead of "Dublin Heuston", the calculator recognises your input. However, if you misspell a stop, the calculator may not recognise the input.

Will the calculator expand to include the Cork commuter zone?

Currently, there are no plans to expand to Cork's commuter zone. However, this is subject to change.
